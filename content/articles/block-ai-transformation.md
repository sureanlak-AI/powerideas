> **TL;DR**: Block (formerly Square) cut 40% of its workforce because AI made it possible. Owen Jennings explains: engineers using AI tools are 10-100x more productive, so you need fewer of them. The company restructured around AI agents, eliminated layers of management, and now designers and PMs ship code directly. The key insight? Your competitive moat is understanding something that's hard for others to understand — everything else gets "vibe coded away."

## The Decision

Block made headlines when it cut roughly 40% of its workforce. The reason, candidly stated: AI.

Owen Jennings, business lead at Block overseeing product operations and customer support across Square, Cash App, and Afterpay, lays it out clearly:

> "There's been this correlation between the number of folks at a company and the output from the company for decades and decades. I think that basically broke."

What they were seeing: one or two engineers with the right AI tools could be 10, 20, even 100x more productive. The old equation — more people = more output — no longer held.

## The Restructuring

This wasn't a financial decision. It was a structural one.

**What changed:**
- Development teams were completely reorganized — fewer layers, larger spans
- Massive reduction in meetings
- Shift from linear workflows to parallel AI agent automation
- Designers and PMs now ship PRs directly using tools like Builderbot
- Autonomous PR merging with AI oversight

**What stayed the same:**
- Outbound sales, account management — human relationship roles
- Core focus on reliability, compliance, and customer trust

## The AI Stack

Block isn't just using ChatGPT. They've built internal systems:

- **Goose**: A proactive agent harness that routes automations across products
- **Moneybot**: AI chatbot for personalized financial help in Cash App
- **Manager bot**: AI assistant for Square merchants
- **Builderbot**: Tool that lets non-engineers ship code
- **G2**: Internal AI development tool

The vision: deterministic workflows automated end-to-end. Customer support chatbots, AI phone support, automated risk and compliance decisions.

## Generative UI: The Next Frontier

One of the most interesting insights: Block is moving away from static user interfaces.

Instead of building one UI that looks the same for everyone, they're building systems that generate dynamic interfaces tailored to individual users. The UI adapts to what you need, when you need it.

This is a fundamental shift in how we think about product design. The interface isn't designed — it's generated.

## The Moat Question

The interviewer asks the critical question: what's your competitive advantage when AI can code anything?

> "The biggest moat is going to be which companies understand something that's super hard for other people to understand."

For Block, that's understanding how sellers and buyers participate in the economy. They're building "world models" — deep understanding of their customers and operations — that improve through feedback loops.

If your answer to "what's your moat?" is "I don't know," then you might get vibe-coded away.

## Key Takeaways

1. **The people-output correlation broke.** More people ≠ more output when AI amplifies individual productivity by 10-100x.

2. **Restructure around AI, not around people.** Block didn't just fire people — they redesigned how work happens.

3. **Non-engineers can ship code now.** Designers and PMs using AI tools can contribute directly to the product.

4. **Your moat is understanding, not execution.** When everyone can execute, what you understand becomes the differentiator.

5. **Generative UI is coming.** Static interfaces for everyone will look archaic within a few years.

6. **The future: humans as editors, not creators.** Block envisions systems running hundreds of times a day with humans reviewing, not building.
